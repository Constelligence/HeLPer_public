## Query 20: What are the crane standing or supporting conditions of the sitting of mobile cranes?

**References:** LD (2017) Code of Practice for Safe Use of Mobile Cranes

### Response 1

Based on regulatory and technical provisions under **OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1402** and **ASME B30.5 Section 5‑1.1**, the **standing or supporting conditions for siting (positioning) mobile cranes** are as follows:

---

### 1. **Ground Bearing Capacity**
- **Requirement (OSHA 1926.1402(a))**:  
  The ground conditions *must be firm, drained, and graded to a level sufficient to support the crane and its load with no significant subsidence*.  
- **Verification**: The controlling entity (typically the prime contractor) must ensure ground-bearing pressure is evaluated before crane setup.
- **Engineer Duty**: Load-bearing pressure (`P = W_total / A_support`) must not exceed the rated capacity of the ground or supporting structure.

---

### 2. **Supporting Surfaces**
- **Acceptable conditions (ASME B30.5‑1.1.2)**:
  - Crane must be set up on **level, stable, and uniformly supported** surfaces.
  - If the surface is sloped, the manufacturer’s specified maximum permissible slope must not be exceeded.
  - Cribbing, mats, plates, or other supporting materials must distribute load uniformly and prevent sinking or tilting.

---

### 3. **Outrigger and Crawler Support**
- **Outrigger Use (ASME B30.5‑1.3 & OSHA 1926.1404(q))**:
  - Outriggers must be fully extended or set per manufacturer’s load chart configuration.
  - Supporting pads or mats shall be of sufficient strength and area to spread loads to safe ground bearing levels.
- **Crawler Setup**:
  - Crawler tracks must rest evenly on the ground; no voids or unstable fill allowed underneath.
  - For soft ground, engineered mats must provide uniform bearing.

---

### 4. **Prohibited Conditions**
- No crane setup on:
  - Excavations, trenches, or uncompacted fill without engineered verification.
  - Frozen or saturated ground that may thaw or settle under load.
  - Utility vaults or underground structures without load capacity validation.

---

### 5. **Verification and Documentation**
- **Pre‑lift documentation required**:
  - **Ground Bearing Report / GRI (Ground Reaction Information)** by a competent person or engineer.
  - **Crane Setup Drawing** showing outrigger layout, mat dimensions, and exclusion zone.
- **Competent Person (OSHA 1926.1404(q)(1))** must verify the supporting condition during crane assembly and setup.

---

### 6. **Regulatory References**
- **OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1402** – Ground conditions.  
- **OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1404(q)** – Outrigger and supporting surfaces.  
- **ASME B30.5‑1.1.2, 1.3** – Site preparation and supporting surface.  
- **Manufacturer’s Operations Manual** – Always supersedes general practice in case of conflict.

---

✅ **Summary Compliance Principle:**  
> *A mobile crane must be positioned on firm, level, and adequately supported ground, verified by engineering data, with load-distribution measures in place (mats/cribbing) to prevent settlement or instability.*

---

### **1. Ground Bearing Capacity**
- **OSHA 1926.1402(b)** requires the controlling entity (usually the general contractor) to ensure the ground conditions are sufficient to **support the crane and its load, per manufacturer's specifications**.

  - Crane gross weight with counterweights installed  
  - Dynamic loads during slewing and braking  
  - Outrigger reaction loads or crawler track pressures

**Practical Compliance Benchmark:**  
Typical mobile crane outrigger reaction can exceed **250–350 kN per outrigger** on large units; site must be verified through geotechnical testing or engineering calculation.

---

### **2. Firm, Level, and Prepared Surface**
- **ASME B30.5-2018, Section 5-1.4.2**: The crane must be level within 1% slope (manufacturer’s allowable tolerance) before lifting.  
- Surfaces must be compacted, graded, and free from subsidence risk.  
- Ice, mud, voids, or uncompacted fill must be removed or stabilized.

---

### **3. Use of Supporting Mats, Blocking, or Steel Plates**
- OSHA and ASME require **adequate support under outriggers or tracks** to spread load over a larger area.
- Mats or plates must be:
  - Structurally capable of withstanding maximum anticipated load without excessive deflection  
  - Placed on stable ground, flat, and fully supporting the outrigger pad or crawler track

**OSHA 1926.1404(h)(3)**: Outriggers must be deployed per manufacturer instructions with adequate cribbing.

---

### **4. Proximity & Hazard Clearance**
- **OSHA 1926.1411 & 1926.1408**: Siting must maintain safe clearance from power lines, excavations, unstable slopes, underground utilities, and adjacent structures.  
- Underground hazards: The controlling entity must locate and mark underground voids (culverts, pits, tanks) before crane arrival.

---

### **5. Weather & Environmental Adjustments**
- Wind, rain, frost, or flooding can reduce ground bearing capacity.
- Operations must stop if surface stability is compromised (manufacturer advisory + engineered judgment).
