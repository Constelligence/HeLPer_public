*   Conducting functional checks to ensure that the equipment/machine is in good working condition
*   Documenting any issues found during pre-delivery check for further corrective action

## Query 6: What are the Non-destructive tests by qualified personnel?

**References:** CIC (2010) Guidelines on Safety of Tower Cranes

### Response 1

Non-destructive testing (NDT) refers to a wide range of techniques used to inspect, evaluate, and detect material defects or properties in various materials without causing damage to them. These tests are performed by qualified personnel who have specialized training and experience in the respective NDT methods. Here are some common non-destructive tests:

1. **Visual Inspection (VT):** A visual examination of an object or structure using the naked eye or a magnifying glass to detect surface defects, such as cracks, dents, or corrosion.

2. **Liquid Penetrant Testing (PT):** Also known as dye penetrant testing, this method involves applying a liquid chemical to the surface of a material. The liquid seeps into any cracks or pores and is then removed by cleaning the surface. A developer is applied next, which brings the penetrant back to the surface, making it visible.

3. **Magnetic Particle Testing (MT):** This test is used on ferromagnetic materials. It involves applying a magnetic field to the material, creating lines of magnetic force that interact with any surface or near-surface defects within the material.

4. **Ultrasonic Testing (UT):** Uses high-frequency sound waves to examine the interior of a material for defects like cracks and voids. The test sends ultrasonic pulses into the part being tested; these echoes return as signals when they hit surfaces, providing information on the internal structure of the material.

5. **Radiography (RT):** This involves using ionizing radiation (like X-rays or gamma rays) to produce images of the internal structure of materials. It is used for a wide range of applications, including detecting defects in welds and evaluating the integrity of pipelines and other critical infrastructure.

6. **Acoustic Emission Testing (AET):** Monitors the high-frequency sounds emitted by a material when it undergoes stress or failure. This test can detect tiny changes within a material before they become visible or cause significant damage.

7. **Eddy Current Testing (ECT):** Uses electromagnetic induction to inspect materials, particularly metals. It involves sweeping an eddy current probe over the surface of a part being tested; any defects or variations in the material's properties will affect the response of the probe.

8. **Leak Detection:** Involves various techniques such as soap and water testing, halide gas testing, or using ultrasonic leak detectors to find leaks in pipes and other systems.

9. **Hardness Testing (HT):** Measures the hardness (resistance to indentation) of a material's surface. This can indicate the material's properties, including its strength and durability.

10. **Optical Emission Testing:** Uses optical instruments or cameras to capture images of the internal structure of materials, often under controlled lighting conditions that enhance contrast between different parts of the inspected object.

Each NDT method has specific applications and is used for different types of inspections based on the material properties, geometry, and the types of defects expected. The choice of test depends on factors like the part's location, its function, the level of inspection required, and environmental conditions.
